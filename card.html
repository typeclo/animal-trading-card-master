Hawksbill Sea Turtle

The Hawksbill Turtle is one of the smallest species of turtle.
Their narrow pointed beak is a specialized feeding tool,
much like that of a bird of prey.

  • Scientific name: Eretmochelys imbricata
  • Average Length: 2.5 feet
  • Average lifespan: 30 to 50 years
  • Habitat: Warm, tropical coastline waters

The hawksbill is one of the smaller sea turtles.
Head is narrow and has 2 pairs of prefrontal scales.
Carapace is bony without ridges and has large,
over-lapping scutes present and has 4 lateral scutes.
Carapace is elliptical in shape. Flippers have 2 claws.
The carapace is orange, brown or yellow and hatchlings
are mostly brown with pale blotches on scutes.
